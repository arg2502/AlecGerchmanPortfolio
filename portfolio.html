Syntripetal: 3D Unity Game

Syntripetal is a "rollie-pollie" platformer created in Unity 3D. The player rolls one of five balls through a set of levels designed to both entertain and challenge the player, while trying to collect items and beat the course quickly in order to get the highest score.

Chupacabra: 3D OpenGL Game

Chupacabra is a game created in OpenGL and programmed in C++ using Visual Studio 2013. The player plays as Carlos who is riding on his goat through a canyon when suddenly, very round chupacabras start chasing him. The player's goal is to throw objects at these very bouncy chupacabras to knock them off balance before they can get to Carlos.

This game was an eight week long project made with a group of four people. The game engine used, which rendered primitive objects and models onto a screen, was provided by the professor. Most of the work was divided amongst the group members. My main job was to make the chupacabras collide and bounce off all other objects.

Project: Gridlock: Board Game

Project: Gridlock is a turn based, deck building, strategy board game. Two players face off and try to infiltrate their enemy’s territory and capture the keys to their nuclear launch code. The gameplay is similar to chess. The players move around the board, one piece per block. Instead of one king however, the players have two “key” pieces that they must protect and are required to use. When a player loses both keys, they lose.

Different from chess, the players don’t have to use all of their pieces. They must choose which pieces to use depending on their strategy. This was a five week long class project made in a group of four people. We all collaborated on designing and balancing the pieces and board.

Plot Twister: Card Game

Plot Twister is a party card game focused on creating stories based on the cards in your hand and is designed for 3 to 6 players. Players use the different types of cards they are dealt, such as Characters, Rising Action, Conflict, and Resolution, to create a riveting story while also strategizing when to play cards. The object of the game is to collect the most cards. A player collects cards by finishing a story.

This game was an eight week long project with a team consisting of six people. With such an arbitrary concept of players creating the story, this game went through many revisions and balancing to try and make it fit into the concept of a game. Being one of my more difficult projects, it was definitely an important lesson in finding out what a game was.

Town Square: 3D Unity Project

As part of a project involving Autonomous Agents, this Town Square is a solo project is an environment built in Unity filled with two different types of Artificial Intelligence.

The "Pedestrians" are the blue capsules and the "Cars" are the orange blocks. The Pedestrians are designed to simply follow one of two invisibles paths. The Cars are designed to follow a set path as well, but they are also designed to slow down when a Pedestrian is walking in front of them. This is not a game and you do not control a character. You simply switch camera angles through all the Autonomous Agents to see how they behave.

Super Space Shooter Time is the remastering of an older game, Projectile Heck. Projectile Heck: 2D Game

Projectile Heck is an old style 8-bit arcade shooter designed for mobile phones. It was originally intended to be more of a “bullet hell” styled game, but the final product more so resembled a simple space shooter. The object of the game is to control a ship by tapping on the screen where you want it to go, shoot down enemy ships, and try to get a high score.

This game was a passion project made with a group of three people. It was the first game I ever made with a group of people. My main goal was to compose music and add sound effects. I also helped design and debugg the main code, which was written in Java. Projectile Heck is now available on the Google Play Store..
